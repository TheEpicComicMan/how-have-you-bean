How Bean Nye Was Grown


Germination:

Following careful research, I found that the best way to germinate my bean seed was through the "Ziploc Method". The plan called for the bean(s) to be put inside a damp paper towel then placed inside a Ziploc for two to three days. Following this process the seeds germinated in no time.

-----Time To Grow-----


Soil:

Regular Potting Soil


Container:

Large pot followed by five small pots


Location:

Outside then under a lamp


Why?

I originally chose to put it outside because I thought the natural sunlight would be beneficial for the plant. When there was no growth, I figured it was because of competition for resources so I put all five beans in separate pots. I though I had solved the problem. When there was still no growth I moved one bean plant inside and put it under a lamp for 24 hours. I ignored the rest of the beans and put my sole focus into this one bean. I worked hard to keep this bean watered and under the lamp. The bean started to grow faster than I thought it could. Over just one day, the bean must have grown upwards of three centimeters. I could not believe it. By the time the last bean check- before the final-came around, this bean was at 42 centimeters. I thought it could never get there. Sadly, after that bean check the top of Bean Nye died so I was forced to remove it. It has slightly grown back but not quite back to 42cm. My best measurements place it at 40cm.

Life Cycle of Bean Nye


Germination

For the fastest germination, soak the seeds in water for 12-24 hours prior to planting. This opens up the embryo and lets the roots start forming once in dirt.

Seedlings

The shoots are bent over at first, and begin a process known as phototropism, which is the process during which the stem straightens and grows upward, toward the light. Two small leaves are produced, which continue to increase in size as the root system develops more fully.

Maturing Plants

Kidney beans take approximately six weeks to mature from seedlings and begin producing flowers. During this period, the root systems mature and anchor into the soil of their permanent growing area, and the plant produces multiple leaves from the main stem.

Fruit Production

Flowers begin to form once the plant has matured, from the junction of stem and leaves. Once pollinated, the flower withers and dies, revealing a tiny kidney bean pod.
